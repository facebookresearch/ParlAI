7 ## Abstract
8 
9 The use of deep pre-trained bidirectional transformers has led to remarkable progress in anumber of applications (Devlin et al., 2018). For tasks that make pairwise comparisons between sequences, matching a given input with a corresponding label, two approaches are common: Cross-encoders performing full self-attention over the pair and Bi-encoders encoding the pair separately. The former often performs better, but is too slow for practical use. In this work, we develop a new transformer architecture, the Poly-encoder, that learns global rather than token level self-attention features. We perform a detailed comparison of all three approaches, including what pre-training and fine-tuning strategies work best. We show our models achieve state-of-the-art results on three existing tasks; that Poly-encoders are faster than Cross-encoders and more accurate than Bi-encoders; and that the best results are obtained by pre-training on large datasets similar to the downstream tasks.

13 ## Code and models
14 
15 Below we give details about available code and models:
16 - pretrained transformers on Reddit and Wikipedia + Toronto Books that can be
17  used as a base for pretraining
18  - pretrained models fine tuned on ConvAI2 for the bi-encoder and poly-encoder

20  interact in real time with 100k candidates (which are provided.)

55 python -u examples/train_model.py \

57  --batchsize 512 -pyt convai2 \

59  --warmup_updates 100 --lr-scheduler-patience 0 \
60  --lr-scheduler-decay 0.4 -lr 5e-05 --data-parallel True \
61  --history-size 20 --label-truncate 72 --text-truncate 360 \
62  --num-epochs 10.0 --max_train_time 200000 -veps 0.5 -vme 8000 \
63  --validation-metric accuracy --validation-metric-mode max \
64  --save-after-valid True --log_every_n_secs 20 --candidates batch \
65  --dict-tokenizer bpe --dict-lower True --optimizer adamax \
66  --output-scaling 0.06 \
67  --variant xlm --reduction-type mean --share-encoders False \
68  --learn-positional-embeddings True --n-layers 12 --n-heads 12 \
