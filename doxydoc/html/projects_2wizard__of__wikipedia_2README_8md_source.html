5 The Wizard of Wikipedia is an open-domain dialogue task for training agents
6 that can converse knowledgably about open-domain topics!
7 A detailed description may
8 be found in [Dinan et al. (ICLR 2019)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1811.01241).
9 
10 ## Abstract
11 
12 In open-domain dialogue intelligent agents should exhibit the use of knowledge,
13 however there are few convincing demonstrations of this to date. The most
14 popular sequence to sequence models typically "generate and hope" generic
15 utterances that can be memorized in the weights of the model when mapping from
16 input utterance(s) to output, rather than employing recalled knowledge as
17 context. Use of knowledge has so far proved difficult, in part because of the
18 lack of a supervised learning benchmark task which exhibits knowledgeable open
19 dialogue with clear grounding. To that end we collect and release a large
20 dataset with conversations directly grounded with knowledge retrieved from
21 Wikipedia. We then design architectures capable of retrieving knowledge,
22 reading and conditioning on it, and finally generating natural responses. Our
23 best performing dialogue models are able to conduct knowledgeable discussions
24 on open-domain topics as evaluated by automatic metrics and human evaluations,
25 while our new benchmark allows for measuring further improvements in this
26 important research direction.

78 ## End-to-End generative
79 
80 You can evaluate the pretrained End-to-end generative models via:
81 
82  python examples/eval_model.py \
83  -bs 64 -t wizard_of_wikipedia:generator:random_split \
84  -mf models:wizard_of_wikipedia/end2end_generator/model
85 
86 
87 This produces the following metrics:
88 
89  {'f1': 0.1717, 'ppl': 61.21, 'know_acc': 0.2201, 'know_chance': 0.02625}
90 
91 This differs slightly from the results in the paper, as it is a recreation trained
92 from scratch for public release.
93 
94 You can also evaluate the model on the unseen topic split too:
95 
96  python examples/eval_model.py \
97  -bs 64 -t wizard_of_wikipedia:generator:topic_split \
98  -mf models:wizard_of_wikipedia/end2end_generator/model
99 
100 This will produce:
101 
102  {'f1': 0.1498, 'ppl': 103.1, 'know_acc': 0.1123, 'know_chance': 0.02496}
103 
104 You can also interact with the model with:
